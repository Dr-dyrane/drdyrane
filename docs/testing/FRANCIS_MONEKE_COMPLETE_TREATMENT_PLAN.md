## 🎯 **WORKING DIAGNOSIS**

**Primary:** Lichen Simplex Chronicus (ICD-10: L28.0)  
**Secondary:** Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (ICD-10: L81.0)

### **Pathophysiology:**
Chronic itch → Scratching → Skin thickening (lichenification) → Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation → Self-perpetuating itch-scratch cycle

### **Key Triggers Identified:**
1. Heat and sweating
2. Exercise
3. Collar friction
4. Unconscious scratching
5. Inadequate barrier repair (irregular moisturizing)

---

## 💊 **COMPREHENSIVE TREATMENT PLAN**

### **PHASE 1: INDUCTION (Control Inflammation)**
**Goal:** Flatten lichenified plaque and break itch cycle

#### **A. Neck (Thicker Skin)**
**Rx: Mometasone Furoate 0.1% Cream**
- **Dose:** Thin layer to affected neck areas
- **Frequency:** Once nightly
- **Duration:** 14 days ONLY
- **Instructions:**
  - Use very small amount (pea-sized for entire neck)
  - Apply only to dark thickened areas
  - Do NOT use on face
  - STOP after 2 weeks (risk of skin atrophy)

#### **B. Face (Thin Skin)**
**Rx: Tacrolimus 0.03% Ointment**
- **Dose:** Thin layer to affected facial areas
- **Frequency:** Twice daily (morning and night)
- **Duration:** 3 weeks
- **Benefits:**
  - Safe for face (no skin thinning)
  - Reduces inflammation and itch
  - Steroid-sparing agent
- **Expected side effect:** Mild burning/warmth for first 3-5 days (NORMAL)

---

### **PHASE 2: BARRIER REPAIR (Essential for Prevention)**
**Goal:** Restore skin barrier to prevent relapse

**Rx: Continue existing moisturizer**
- **CeraVe Moisturizing Cream** OR
- **La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+M**

**Application schedule:**
1. **Morning** (after washing face)
2. **Immediately after bathing** (within 3 minutes)
3. **Before bed**

**Sites:** Face, neck, and surrounding skin

**Critical point:** Irregular moisturizing = guaranteed relapse

---

### **PHASE 3: ANTI-ITCH CONTROL**
**Goal:** Reduce itch perception and unconscious scratching

**Rx: Levocetirizine 5mg Tablet**
- **Dose:** 1 tablet
- **Frequency:** Once nightly
- **Duration:** 10 days
- **Purpose:**
  - Reduce itch perception
  - Sedation reduces nighttime scratching
  - More effective than cetirizine

---

### **PHASE 4: TRIGGER MANAGEMENT**

#### **A. Heat/Sweat Control**
**Instructions:**
- Avoid very hot showers → Use **lukewarm water**
- After exercise → **Cool shower immediately**
- Pat skin dry (do NOT rub)
- Apply moisturizer within 3 minutes of drying

#### **B. Friction Reduction**
**Instructions:**
- Wear **loose-neck clothing**
- Avoid rough collars
- Avoid chains/jewelry touching neck
- Use **soft cotton fabrics**
- Consider collarless shirts during healing phase

#### **C. Behavioral Itch Control**
**When itching starts - DO NOT SCRATCH**

**Instead:**
1. Press area firmly with palm (10 seconds)
2. Apply moisturizer immediately
3. Use **cold compress** for 5 minutes
4. Keep fingernails **short** at all times

---

### **PHASE 5: PIGMENTATION RECOVERY**
**Timeline:** AFTER inflammation controlled (4-6 weeks)

**Current approach:** NO aggressive bleaching during active inflammation

**After 4-6 weeks, if pigmentation persists:**
- **Azelaic Acid 15-20% cream** (once nightly)
- **Niacinamide 10% serum** (twice daily)
- **Mild chemical exfoliation** (glycolic acid 5-10%)

**Important:** Pigmentation will fade naturally once itch-scratch cycle is broken
