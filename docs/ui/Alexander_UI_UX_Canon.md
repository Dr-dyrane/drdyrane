# 🧊 **Alexander UI/UX Canon — v1.0 (Frozen)**

*Light-numbered. Memory-friendly. Non-negotiable.*

---

## 1. Purpose First

No UI without a job.
If it doesn’t reduce friction, fear, or time — it doesn’t exist.

---

## 2. Best Default Wins

Each screen has one opinionated layout.
Users may override, never decide upfront.

---

## 3. Reveal Gradually

Show only what’s needed *now*.
Details appear with intent (hover, focus, drill-down).

---

## 4. One Screen, One Action

Every view has a single dominant action.
Everything else steps back.

---

## 5. State Is Design

Loading, empty, error, partial — all intentional.
Nothing goes blank. Nothing fails silently.

---

## 6. Calm Feedback

Always show progress.
Never make users wonder if it worked.

---

## 7. Color Has Meaning

Color = state, not decoration.
If it doesn’t change meaning, remove it.

---

## 8. Familiar Navigation

Predictable beats clever.
No surprise routes. No hidden primaries.

---

## 9. Stress-Ready

Design for hurry, emotion, bad networks.
If it fails under stress, it failed.

---

## 10. Dashboard = Control

Dashboards monitor and intervene.
Signals over charts. Action over beauty.

---

## 11. Ask Before Assuming

Ambiguity pauses the system.
Questions beat inventions.

---

## 12. Code Mirrors UX

Explicit logic. Clear state. No magic.
If it can’t be explained, it’s wrong.

---

## 13. Small, Clean, Scalable

Ship the smallest coherent experience.
Architect quietly for growth.

---

## 14. Every Flow Ends

Clear completion. Clear next step.
No dead ends.

---

## 15. Respect Intelligence

No patronizing copy.
Calm. Direct. Confident.

---

## 16. Reveal on Hover

Secondary info stays hidden until intent appears.
Silence by default.

---

## 17. Motion Explains

Animations show cause → effect.
Slide, fade, morph — never bounce.

---

## 18. Preserve Spatial Memory

Transitions show where you came from.
No teleporting.

---

## 19. Focused Forms

One input at a time.
Dim the world. Think with the user.

---

## 20. Premium Lightness

Transparent layers. Soft blur. Subtle depth.
Glass, not gloss.

---

## 21. Depth Over Color

Contrast through layers, not paint.
Color stays semantic.

---

## 22. Touch Feels Equal

Mouse, touch, trackpad all feel intentional.
Hover never required.

---

## 23. Micro = Craft

Small transitions signal quality.
Milliseconds matter.

---

## 24. White Space Is Luxury

Silence is intentional.
Noise is expensive.

---

## 25. Underpaid Effect

The product should feel expensive, calm, effortless.
Never flashy. Never trying.

---

## 26. Time Is Designed

Acknowledge instantly.
Refine progressively.

---

## 27. Edge States Matter

Errors and emptiness are beautiful, clear, useful.

---

## 28. Feels Touchable

Buttons press. Sliders weigh. Cards resist.
Flat doesn’t mean dead.

---

## 29. Ruthless Hierarchy

Only one thing leads.
Everything else yields.

---

## 30. Type Is Interface

Size, weight, spacing communicate authority.
Weak type = cheap product.

---

## 31. Pause After Action

Confirm before moving on.
Confidence lives in that beat.

---

## 32. Intentional Friction

Friction protects important actions.
Never block common ones.

---

## 33. No Visual Debt

No “temporary” styles.
Polish compounds.

---

## 34. Never Panic

Even in emergencies, the UI stays composed.

---

## 35. One Step Ahead

Pre-fill. Predict. Anticipate.
Great UX feels like mind-reading.

---

## 36. Consistency Wins

Patterns repeat.
Novelty is rare.

---

## 37. Endings Matter

Clear success. Clear next step.
Certainty is the goal.

---

## 38. Invisible Onboarding

Teach by use, not instruction.
If it needs explaining, redesign it.

---

## 39. Quiet Authority

Confident. Minimal. Non-defensive.
Shown, not declared.

---

## 40. Timeless Screens

If it won’t age well, it doesn’t ship.

---

## 41. Reuse First

Existing UI is default.
New components must earn existence.

---

## 42. One Visual Language

Colors, spacing, motion, copy stay consistent.
No foreign accents.

---

## 43. Mutate, Don’t Multiply

Variants over new components.
Proliferation is failure.

---

## 44. Tokens Rule

No hardcoded values.
If it’s not tokenized, it’s not real.

---

## 45. Words Are UI

Same action → same words everywhere.

---

## 46. Color Loyalty

New colors require meaning, not taste.

---

## 47. Familiarity Is a Feature

Learning compounds only when patterns repeat.

---

## 48. Refine Quietly

Mature products evolve without announcing it.
